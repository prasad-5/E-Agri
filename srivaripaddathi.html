SRIVARISAGU PROCESS

VARINARU PLANTING

Only two leaves of 8 to 12 days old should be planted. Due to this, a large number of shoots will be produced. The roots will spread well.


Careful planting

The plant should be carefully removed from the narumadi, along with the root, mud and seed, and should be pressed on the surface of the field. Do not plant deeply. This way, the plant will not be subjected to severe stress which is natural during cultivation, and it will survive, grow quickly and produce a large number of shoots.


Planting far apart

It should be planted so that there is a distance of 25 cm from the plant to the plant. In the lands with high soil fertility, it can also be planted dry.

WEED CONTROL

Weeds are more of a problem as we keep water in the field. For weed prevention, once in 10 days after planting, with a rotary/kono weeder, shake the soil three more times after 10 days, the weeds will disappear in the soil. Use of rotary/cono weeder provides better oxygenation to the roots. As a result, microorganisms develop and fix nitrogen. Farmers' experience shows that when working with rotary/cono weeder more than twice, the yield is higher by 2 tons per hectare.



Ownership of water

Water management should be done very carefully. The field should be wet but the water should not stand. If the water is too much, a drain should be arranged every 2 meters.
