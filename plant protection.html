What is comprehensive plant protection?

Comprehensive plant protection means that the ecological balance is not disturbed on the roofs The various pests that come in are periodically assessed and the crops Finally, plant protection through low-cost farming methods, mechanical methods, biological control methods without causing any damage Achieving higher yields by using medicines. In other words, keeping in mind the environment, in order to achieve high yields in the fields, the pests that are harmful to the crop are controlled from crossing the damage limit. Comprehensive plant protection is the method followed to protect the beneficial insects.

Why comprehensive plant protection?
  • To protect the natural enemies of insects
  • To reduce the use of insecticides.
  • To reduce the cost of cultivation.
  • to maintain the balance of the atmosphere
  • To produce quality healthy crops.
  • In order to prevent increased resistance to pesticides in pests.
  • In order to avoid erosion of resistance to pests in crop varieties.
  • In order to prevent the growth of the offspring of insects that have died due to the spraying of chemical pesticides.
  • To reduce human cancer, eye diseases, stomach ulcers, and skin and heart diseases caused by chemical drugs.

    At the base of the infected leaves, the insect swarms in the early stages and such leaves should be easily identified, picked up and destroyed from a distance.

    The bollworm should pluck the bolls as soon as expected and co-destroy them with the insects. If the intensity is high, spray Monocrotophos (1.5 ml/lit of water) or Chlorpyriphos (2 ml/lit of water).

    In September and October, when the soil is high during the sowing stage of the crop, and the night temperature is less than 22 degrees Celsius for about four to five days in a row, the fungus disease is more expected. Since there are no fully resistant varieties of the disease, the infected seedlings can be cut off and destroyed and the fungicide called carbendazim can be sprayed twice in a span of 10 days depending on the intensity of the twigs.

    Sowing individual varieties of bollworms can reduce the severity of bollworm and mould pests.

    Deep ploughing of the soil in the summer can destroy insects, fungi and bacteria in the coarse stage.

    Rainwater should not be stored in the field.

    Sorghum crop

    Sorghum crop harvesting

    Among the pests that cause severe damage to the jowar crop are the moth-borer, panicle, lice, matchworm and molestation.

    A fly that sheds the crop causes extensive damage within a month from the time of sowing. The stem borer comes from two weeks after sowing to the time of harvesting. As a result of biting, the bud dries up and gradually dies, and the dried buds come easily when plucked, causing extensive damage.

    If you sow within 7-10 days of the kharif season and the last week of September to the first week of October in rabi, the fly can be avoided to a great extent.

    Sorghum crop can also be protected from flies by using more than 1-1.5 kg of seed per acre while sowing the seed and sowing the plants closely in the fields and removing the infected plants.

    Plants that have been infected by a fly or stem borer must be removed from the field and destroyed.

    Harvesting close to the ground while harvesting the crop and destroying the crop residues in the soil can destroy the coagulation stages of insects such as stem borer and caterpillar.

    Deep ploughing in summer can lead to the destruction of insect repellents in the soil, in the weeds, in the soil, under the influence of birds or by the heat of the sun. This can reduce the infestation of the pest in the upcoming crop.

    Sowing the same type of sorghum on time, at the same time, in large areas can reduce the infestation of muvva fly, panicle, and matchworms.

    The use of sorghum as an intercropping can not only reduce the intensity of stem borer by up to 50 per cent, but also increase the yield of the crop by 10-12 per cent.
