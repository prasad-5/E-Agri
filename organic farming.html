Organic farming

Organic farming can be described as a naturally environmentally friendly bio-based system. Organic farming enhances the diversity of organisms, the different stages of organisms and the performance of microorganisms in the month. Protecting the soil
Soil Conservation

Covering the soil or the soil with waste materials can protect the soil without any financial loss from sunlight, wind and rainwater harvesting, and without any damage to the soil.

Ploughing as per the need
the least tillage

Excessive and deep ploughing not only exposes the soil to erosion, but also reduces the number of microorganisms and planktons (Flora, Fana) in the month. Therefore, the soil should be ploughed less only to the extent required.

Practice of mixed agriculture
Mixed agriculture

Agriculture or the cultivation of crops and animal husbandry must be followed to help each other in every possible way.

Mixed crops in organic farming

In organic farming, mixed crops, multiple or multicropping and intercrops must be cultivated properly. Different crop plants absorb the nutrients they want from different depths of the soil. If some crops are cultivated together as mixed crops, the crops will grow well. So two or more varieties of crops can be cultivated depending on their needs. Moreover, mixed and multi-crops can reduce the infestation of pests. Crop rotation plays an important role in keeping the soil healthy and in the natural functioning of microorganisms.
