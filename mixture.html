Precautions in the preparation of various mi srams

Preparation of neem solution:

  • Take neem seeds, dry them, put a kilo of powder in a thin cloth bag, tie the lid and soak it overnight in 20 liters of water.
  • The bag soaked in this way should be squeezed as many times as possible. By doing that, the azadirachtin root extract in the powder will come into the infusion. After squeezing completely, the bag containing the pulp should be thrown away. In this way, neem infusion is prepared.
  • 20 grams of soap powder should be added to this solution and dissolved well. This mixed potion should be filtered through a thin cloth. Otherwise, the substances remaining in the potion will get stuck in the sprayer nozzle and the spraying will be interrupted.
  • In this way, the required amount of solution can be prepared and sprayed in all crops for the prevention of all insects. Good results can be obtained if the stage of insect larvae is observed and sprayed immediatel

How to prepare Bordeaux mixture and Bordeaux paste?

Preparation of Bordeaux mixture: 1% Bordeaux mixture is prepared by mixing Mylatutham, (copper sulphate), lime and water in the ratio of 1:1:100. If quicklime is not available, wet lime can be used, but the above three should be mixed in the ratio of 1:1.5:100. The lime should be free of clay and sand. It is better if the lime found in the markets is in sealed bags. Grind the mylatuthum finely. Before making the mixture tomorrow, the mylatuthum powder should be dissolved in water the day before. Bordomixram can be prepared in two ways.

First method: Take Mylathutthum and lime in the prescribed amount and pour enough water in separate vessels and mix well. Pour Mylathutthum water into the vessel containing lime water. All the vessels should be made of clay, wood or plastic.

Seond method: Mix mylatutthum and lime powder in separate vessels with enough water and pour the water from these two vessels simultaneously into the third vessel.

For example, take 50 liters of water in separate vessels and mix 1 kg of milatutthum in one and 1 kg of lime in the second vessel. Pour the water in these two vessels into the third vessel at the same time.

A test should be done before using the bordeaux mixture. Litmus paper should not turn red when dipped in the mixture. If litmus paper is not found, dip a new blade or knife in the mixture so that copper ore does not reach them.

Bordeaux paste: For this, mix one kg of milututthum powder, 1.5 kg of dry lime, and 13.5 liters of water. It should be tested and used like the Bordeaux mixture. If you apply it to the broken, cut branches, injured parts and tree trunks using a brush, pests will not be expected.

Plant protection against NPV virus:

Among the various integrated plant protection methods, biological control methods are very important. Among the many biological control methods available to us is nuclear polyhedrosis virus (NPV) solution, which can be prepared at low cost in farmer's cotton. NPV is an insect pathogen. A virus. These virus particles enter the digestive tract when the worm eats through the leaves and other parts of the plant, reproduce there and weaken the digestive tract. The virus particles enter through the leaves and other parts of the body, causing damage to all systems and the worm dies. The body of dead insects infected with NPV is swollen, the skin is loose, and when touched, the skin bursts and a thick white liquid like poola comes out. The infected insects crawl to the edges of the plants and die hanging upside down.

The important thing to note here is that the virus produced by any insect can only kill the same type of insect. For example, the virus isolated from the bean green worm did not cause disease to the caterpillar. Therefore, the virus of any species of insect should be used only for the prevention of insects of the same species.

Procedure for preparing green worm NPV:

Collecting larvae:

Collect healthy fourth instar larvae from the crop fields into injection vials or individual larvae into cell wells. If the larvae are small, the virus production will be reduced. If larger larvae are selected, a higher dose of virus will be required to kill them. Therefore, it is better to select a moderate size (1.0-1.5 cm long) larvae.

Susceptibility of larvae to virus diseases::

First soak the chickpea seeds for 6-8 hours and then dry them on a dry cloth. Sprinkle the pure virus solution (mother culture) in proper amount (40L.E per kg seed) on these seeds. This mother culture solution is available from the Department of Agriculture in their Biological Control Laboratory (BCLabs) or some research centers affiliated to the State Agricultural University or some Research Council affiliated to the Indian Council of Agricultural Research. Then the mouth of the bottle should be tightened with cotton. If possible, these bottles should be kept in favorable weather conditions i.e. 25-30 degrees centigrade temperature and 60-70 percent humidity. If necessary, after the 3rd day, these larvae can be fed with normal food i.e. virus-free, soaked cowpeas.

Aggregation of virus larvae:

Virus infected larvae die after 4-5 days. Only larvae should be collected in a bottle. Dead larvae are soft, so there is a possibility of bacterial growth on them and putrefy smell. Therefore, proper care should be taken while removing dead larvae from the bottles. Keeping it in the fridge stops the growth of bacteria and reduces the bad smell.

Purification of virus:

After collecting the virus-infected and dead larvae, equal parts of clean water are added (250 ml of water for 250 larvae), put them in a grinder and grind them until they become a thick liquid. If this solution is filtered through a thin cloth, the body parts of the worms are separated. A thick virus solution remains. The remaining, filtered solution was poured into small tubes and centrifuged at 5000rpm for further purification. 15 minutes at can be put

Bollworm virus can also be produced in the same way as mentioned above, but tender castor leaves should be used instead of soaked chickpeas as bollworm food. The leaves should be dipped in mother culture (lollworm NPV), dried and fed to the larvae.

Techniques in using virus::

  • The NPV solution should be mixed with water only before spraying. Spraying the stored solution will reduce the virulence.
  • The virus drug is effective when it is in the larval stage. Therefore, good results can be achieved if the winged insect is detected early through the sex-attracting baskets and spraying the virus accordingly.
  • It should be noted that the use of appropriate dose of virus solution is important. For example: Greenworm NPV should be used at the rate of 500 L.E. per hectare in onion, 250 L.E. per hectare of bean, sunflower, tomato.
  • Compared to other chemical insecticides, virus medicine works a bit late. Even if insects appear in the field 4-5 days after spraying, the damage caused by them is reduced.
  • If there is a high percentage of large larvae past the 4th stage in the field, NPV. does not work efficiently, so it is better to choose alternative methods.
  • In addition to NPV solution, 1/2 kg jaggery and 100 ml teepal and 50 g robin blue should be sprayed in sufficient water per acre (about 200 litres).
  • The virus solution should be sprayed evenly throughout the plants, especially on the young leaves, pods and seeds. Depending on the severity of the pest, the virus solution should be sprayed 4-5 times in a period of 5-7 days.
  • Alkalinity of spray water should be neutral.
  • Cleanliness is essential when rearing virus-infected larvae. Injection vials or cellwells should be cleaned with a 4 percent Clorox solution before and after use.
Preparation of fresh water tobacco infusion:

To prepare tobacco infusion, 500 grams of tobacco should be soaked in 4.5 liters of water for 24 hours. 320 grams of bar soap powder should be dissolved and mixed with the prepared tobacco infusion. This solution can be mixed with 6-7 times water and sprayed.
